01 | Methodology

From scattered tasks to one performance engine

Every engagement begins with one spine: the conversion path, measurement layer, and page architecture that channels everything into readable decisions.

How I build the system

  • Translate commercial targets into a measurable funnel: actions, stages, and definitions of success.
  • Map demand and competitors, then convert signals into an intent map and landing page requirements.
  • Design the measurement spine: GA4 events, GTM triggers, UTMs, conversion goals, and dashboards.
  • Build acquisition architecture so Search, Shopping, and Performance Max do not overlap or conflict.
  • Connect content creation to real demand: hooks, proof, objections, and refresh cadence.
  • Stabilize: pacing, guardrails, anomaly checks, and a weekly monitoring rhythm.

What makes it fast to operate

  • Small metric set for decisions: leads, cost per lead, qualified lead rate, revenue actions, and page performance.
  • Documentation that makes scaling predictable: naming conventions, audience logic, and decision rules.
  • Pages and ads are built together, so message match stays tight during tests and scaling.
  • Dashboards are structured for management: stop, scale, and next tests become obvious.

05 | SEO

SEO that supports paid, not a checklist

SEO becomes the backbone that lets paid traffic land on pages that already win, and it keeps demand discovery always active.

How the journey is structured

  • Audit Search Console and GA4 to find where the brand does not exist and which queries are worth owning.
  • Design landing pages so each intent group has a dedicated URL, not a generic homepage.
  • Rewrite titles, meta, headings, and internal anchors to match commercial intent and ad messaging.
  • Fix internal linking so visitors move deeper logically into collections and products.
  • Use schema where it adds clarity: product, FAQ, breadcrumbs, local business when relevant.
  • Turn query data into content briefs, angles, and Performance Max creative starting points.

Impact for TechnoMounts

  • Money queries that had no presence moved into consistent visibility.
  • Organic visitors land on the same high intent pages used for ads and shopping.
  • SEO reporting is tied to revenue pages, not only traffic totals.
  • Search data becomes a continuous input for creative and offer optimization.
06 | Automation and AI

Keeping the system light to operate

Automation reduces repeat work. AI accelerates research and drafting while final quality stays controlled through standards and review.

AI in daily work

  • Custom assistants per account to keep tone, offers, and documentation consistent.
  • Surface patterns and anomalies from exports and dashboards to generate starting hypotheses.
  • Draft ad copy, email variants, and landing sections, then refine manually.
  • Maintain prompt libraries for FAQs, product copy, and page structures that respect brand rules.
  • Summarize stakeholder notes into structured action lists and test plans.

Automation in the stack

  • Email flows: onboarding, abandoned cart, reactivation, loyalty, and winback.
  • Lifecycle stages and tagging so leads and customers never sit in unknown buckets.
  • Reusable landing page components and build templates for fast deployment.
  • Automated report refresh through GA4 and Looker Studio for live visibility.
  • Central learnings log updated consistently from campaign notes and tests.
