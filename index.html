SEO and discoverability

Search work focused on structure, intent, and technical health.

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is treated as part of the system, not a separate checklist. The goal is to let paid and organic support each other.

SEO methodology

  • Map search intent to real landing pages instead of generic home or collection destinations.
  • Use structured internal linking between blogs, category pages, and high intent product pages.
  • Apply clear on-page structure: titles, meta descriptions, semantic headings, and FAQ blocks.
  • Run ongoing technical checks on Core Web Vitals, crawl errors, and index coverage.
  • Use Search Console data to guide new content and identify queries that should earn a dedicated page.

How this helped brands

  • For TechnoMounts, SEO uplift unlocked organic impressions and clicks that fed the same landing pages used by paid campaigns.
  • For Haksoss Café, search work made it easier for local audiences to discover the brand when looking for premium café experiences and breakfast options.
  • For Rafiki and Mory Gym, SEO reinforced the campaigns by capturing intent from people researching clinics and gyms before they responded to ads.
AI and automation

Using automation and AI to keep the system efficient.

Automation is used to remove repetitive work so more time can be spent on thinking, creative decisions, and analysis.

How AI is used

  • Build custom GPT style assistants to document processes, generate consistent messaging, and draft structures for emails and ads.
  • Use AI tools to summarise long data exports, pull out patterns, and speed up first pass analysis.
  • Support copy and concept work with AI while keeping final decisions human controlled and strategy led.

Automation in the stack

  • Email flows for onboarding, abandoned carts, reactivation, and loyalty journeys.
  • Basic operational automations such as tagging leads, assigning lifecycle stages, and routing forms into CRM (Customer Relationship Management) pipelines.
  • Templates and snippets to speed up landing page builds and testing so experiments can be launched faster.

How I work

End to end marketing - one person owning the full stack.

Comfortable joining at any point, but the best work happens when the full chain is connected: research, strategy, build, launch, measurement, and iteration.

01
Research and positioning
Market mapping, competitor analysis, audience research, and personas. Clarify where the brand fits, which problems matter most, and how different segments should be prioritised.
02
Strategy and funnel design
Define objectives, channel mix, landing page logic, offers, and lifecycle touchpoints. Build practical funnels tied to numbers, not just messaging.
03
Build and technical setup
Implement campaigns in Google Ads, build landing pages, connect GA4 (Google Analytics 4), Merchant Center, Search Console, pixels, events, and conversion tracking so every action is measurable.
04
Content, creative, and offers
Write ads, page copy, and emails. Brief or design visuals, offers, and loyalty mechanics that match how people actually decide across B2C and B2B influenced journeys.
05
Launch, measurement, and reporting
Launch campaigns, monitor the learning phase, keep dashboards clean, and report using the smallest useful set of metrics so decisions stay clear and focused.
06
Iteration, testing, and scaling
Design tests on audiences, creatives, bids, and pages. Scale what works, cut what does not, and keep the underlying setup healthy instead of chasing short term spikes.
Why this matters in practice

When one person owns the stack, feedback loops are shorter. Insights from analytics can change creative and offer structures quickly. Automation and AI help reduce manual work, keeping more time available for thinking and decision making.
