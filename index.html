01 — Methodology

From single tasks to complete systems

Every engagement begins with one engine. My first job is to design the structure that channels, tracking, and landing pages plug into, so that tests are predictable and performance can be read in minutes, not in hours of guesswork.

Work usually starts with a blank sheet. I map audiences, intent levels, and key actions, then translate that map into campaigns, naming conventions, and landing page logic. This makes sure budgets, search terms, and creative are always tied back to the same performance questions: which journeys create revenue, margin, and pipeline, and which do not.

Once the spine is clear, every asset is judged by how it supports that structure. This is how I keep experiments fast while protecting long term consistency. It also makes it easier for new people to join without rebuilding everything from scratch.

  • Connect acquisition, analytics, and conversion into one performance stack.
  • Use GA4, Google Ads, Search Console, CRM, and landing pages as a single source of truth.
  • Document campaign structures so that tests, scaling, and handovers are predictable.
  • Report using the smallest set of metrics needed for decisions that change revenue and margin.

03 — Flagship project

TechnoMounts UAE — performance ready system

Building a system that took TechnoMounts from almost no search presence to consistently appearing among the top results for high intent hardware queries, while feeding the same structure with Performance Max and Shopping campaigns.

When I joined TechnoMounts, the brand had strong products but almost no organised digital footprint. The first months were dedicated to architecture: mapping eleven landing pages to personas and intent levels, restructuring collections, and creating a clean Google Merchant Center and feed foundation.

In parallel, I used Search Console and GA4 to establish a baseline. Most relevant hardware searches did not show TechnoMounts at all, or buried the brand beyond page three. The goal was simple: own the search real estate for our best products and make sure the same structure could support paid campaigns without duplication or conflict.

05 — SEO

TechnoMounts SEO — from invisible to a visible player

SEO is not treated as a checklist. It is the backbone that lets paid traffic land on pages that already win, and it is how TechnoMounts moved from almost no presence to appearing among the top links for high intent hardware searches.

How the journey was structured

  • Audit Search Console and GA4 to understand where the brand did not exist and which queries were worth owning.
  • Design a landing page map where each key search term group is matched with a specific page, not a generic homepage.
  • Rewrite titles, meta descriptions, and headings to reflect commercial intent and match ad messaging.
  • Clean internal linking across collections, blogs, and product pages so visitors move logically deeper into the site.
  • Implement and maintain product, FAQ, breadcrumb, and local business schema where it adds clarity for search engines.
  • Use Search Console queries to plan new content and detect opportunities where paid and organic results can appear together.

Impact for TechnoMounts

  • Key money terms that previously had no TechnoMounts presence now show the brand in the leading set of results.
  • Organic visitors land on the same eleven high intent pages used for Google Ads and Shopping, improving blended performance.
  • SEO reporting is tied directly to revenue pages, not just to traffic totals, which keeps prioritisation clear.
  • Search data feeds creative and campaign ideas for Performance Max, making every new asset grounded in real queries.
06 — Automation and AI

How I keep the system lighter to operate

AI and automation are not side projects. They are built into how I research, build, launch, and report so more time is spent on decisions and less on repeating manual tasks.

How I use AI in daily work

  • Create custom GPT style assistants per account to keep tone, offers, and documentation consistent.
  • Feed GA4, Search Console, and ad platform exports into AI to surface patterns, anomalies, and starting hypotheses.
  • Generate first drafts of ad copy, angles, and email variants, then refine manually so final quality remains under control.
  • Maintain prompt libraries for landing page sections, FAQs, and product descriptions that respect brand rules.
  • Use AI to summarise stakeholder calls, client notes, and backlog items into structured action lists.

Automation in the stack

  • Build email flows for onboarding, abandoned carts, reactivation, and loyalty tied back to CRM events.
  • Use automated tagging and lifecycle stages so leads and customers never sit in “unknown” buckets.
  • Create reusable landing page components and code blocks that can be dropped into new campaigns quickly.
  • Automate report refreshes through GA4 and Looker Studio so leadership always sees current numbers.
  • Group experiments and learnings in central documents that are updated automatically from campaign notes.

08 — Full stack

End to end marketing owned by one person

This is not a bragging section. It is a practical view of what it means when one person takes responsibility for the full stack: research, build, optimisation, and reporting — even when the work has to be done alone.
Step 1 — Research and positioning

I start by mapping the market: competitors, audience segments, and demand levels. That research is turned into clear positioning, personas, and an order of priority. Everyone knows which segments matter first and which can wait for later phases.

Step 2 — Strategy and funnel design

Objectives are translated into a funnel with channel mix, landing page logic, offers, and lifecycle touchpoints. I document this structure so campaigns, budgets, and creative ideas all point in the same direction and can be compared fairly.

Step 3 — Build and technical setup

I implement campaigns in Google Ads, build landing pages, connect GA4, Merchant Center, Search Console, and pixels, and make sure every key action is measurable. The aim is a clean environment where data is reliable from day one.

Step 4 — Content, creative, and offers

I write ad copy, page copy, and emails, or brief designers with specific angles and objections to answer. Loyalty mechanics and value anchors are planned for both B2C and B2B influenced journeys so that campaigns do more than chase clicks.

Step 5 — Launch, measurement, and reporting

I manage the learning phase, protect budgets while data stabilises, and keep dashboards clean. Reports are structured so leadership can see what to stop, what to scale, and what to test next without reading twenty charts.

Step 6 — Iteration, testing, and scaling

Tests are designed on audiences, creatives, bids, and pages, always tied back to the original funnel map. What works is scaled, what does not is cut, and the underlying structure is protected so new layers can be added without breaking the system.
