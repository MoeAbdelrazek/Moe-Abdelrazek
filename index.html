01 - Methodology

From single tasks to complete systems

The work started as individual tasks - posts, ads, and pages - and matured into owning the full chain from research to reporting. The approach below shows how each engagement is structured today.

Every engagement begins with one clear engine. Channels, tracking, and landing pages are mapped together instead of being handled as separate to do lists. The goal is a setup where tests, scaling, and handovers are predictable rather than improvised.

Phase 1 - Content and campaign tasks
Social posts, simple ads, single objectives

Writing ads and posts, building individual pages, and tracking basic metrics such as clicks and form fills. This phase sharpened copy, creative, and understanding of how people respond in real feeds.

Phase 2 - Channel level systems
Search, social, email, and analytics

Google Ads, Meta, email, and GA4 are connected so budgets, audiences, and events line up. Journeys move from cold traffic into remarketing and lifecycle flows instead of one off pushes.

Phase 3 - Full stack performance engine
Shopify, CRM, reports, and funnels

Campaigns, landing pages, product structures, CRM stages, and dashboards are treated as one architecture. Decisions are made on blended metrics - revenue, margin, and pipeline - instead of isolated platform numbers.

Phase 4 - Continuous improvement
Testing, documentation, and handover

Experiments are logged, creative and landing page variants are documented, and reporting is simplified so leadership can see cause and effect without needing to dig through accounts.

  • Connect acquisition, analytics, and conversion into one performance stack rather than separate vendors.
  • Use GA4, Google Ads, Search Console, CRM, and landing pages as a unified data source.
  • Structure campaigns so that tests, scaling, and handovers are predictable and documented.
  • Report using the fewest metrics needed for decisions that affect revenue, margin, and pipeline.

05 - SEO

SEO as part of the system, not a checklist

SEO is handled as a way to take over a niche, not as a side checklist. The same structure used for paid media guides how pages, content, and technical work are prioritised.

Methodology

  • Map each search intent to a dedicated landing page instead of pushing demand to one generic page.
  • Build internal linking across blogs, collections, and products so visitors naturally move deeper.
  • Maintain clean on page structure - titles, meta descriptions, semantic headings, and structured FAQ blocks.
  • Implement and maintain schema for products, FAQs, breadcrumbs, local business, and articles where relevant.
  • Monitor Core Web Vitals, crawl issues, index coverage, and sitemap health so technical debt does not block growth.
  • Use Search Console queries as a source of truth for new landing pages, blogs, and campaign themes.

Impact in practice

  • TechnoMounts - organic and paid traffic push into the same set of high intent landing pages, lifting both rankings and conversion rates for the niche.
  • Haksoss Cafe - local and brand queries lead to experiences that support loyalty programs and repeat visits instead of just one visit.
  • Rafiki and Mory Gym - SEO captures people researching clinics and gyms, supporting the paid funnel instead of competing with it.

Automation in the stack

  • Reusable landing page components and templates that allow new experiments to launch quickly.
  • Dashboards and reports that refresh automatically so leadership always has a current view of performance.

08 - Full stack

End to end marketing owned by one person

This is not a bragging section. It is a practical view of what it means to take responsibility for the full stack - research, build, optimisation, and reporting - even when work has to be done alone.
Step 1 - Research and positioning

Market mapping, competitor analysis, audience research, and personas. Clarify where the brand fits, what buyers care about most, and which segments to prioritise in the first phase of growth.

Step 2 - Strategy and funnel design

Turn objectives into a funnel with channel mix, landing page logic, offers, and lifecycle touchpoints. Creative ideas are translated into journeys that can be tracked, compared, and optimised.

Step 3 - Build and technical setup

Implement campaigns in Google Ads, build landing pages, connect GA4, Merchant Center, Search Console, pixels, and conversion tracking so every key action is measurable.

Step 4 - Content, creative, and offers

Write ads, page copy, and emails. Brief or design visuals and loyalty mechanics that reflect how people decide in both B2C and B2B influenced journeys.

Step 5 - Launch, measurement, and reporting

Launch campaigns, manage the learning phase, keep dashboards clean, and report with the fewest metrics needed for confident decisions and next moves.

Step 6 - Iteration, testing, and scaling

Design tests on audiences, creatives, bids, and pages. Scale what works, cut what does not, and protect the underlying structure instead of chasing short term spikes. The responsibility is to protect quality and long term performance even when it means more work in the short term.
