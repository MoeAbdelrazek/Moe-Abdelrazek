How my work evolved

From local campaigns to a full performance stack

Each campaign added a layer. Starting with offers and creative, moving into funnels and automation, and then into analytics, attribution, and full account architecture.

Mory Gym - membership acquisition funnel

Fitness · Alexandria

1800 new memberships, campaign reach of 180,000, engagement rate of 18.2 percent, and a calculated ROI of 4,780 percent across the full campaign period.

Learned how to combine creative, offer structure, and automated email flows into a single funnel. Built discipline around persona work, journey mapping, and retention tactics rather than only acquisition.

Rafiki Vets Hospital - lead generation and retention

Healthcare · Animal hospital

Organic metrics included CTR 5 percent, 40 percent bounce rate, and 3.5 percent organic conversion, with 45 keywords in top 10 positions. Paid channels delivered around 2,140 leads across Meta and Google Search during the campaign cycle.

This project deepened my work in automation, CRM integration, and channel attribution. I spent more time wiring tracking, call tracking, and dashboards so that every appointment and enquiry had a clear source.

Haksoss Café - launch and loyalty engine

Hospitality · Premium café concept

Reached 80,800 unique users with an engagement rate of 11.3 percent. Loyalty funnel produced 1,070 sign ups with 6.2 percent conversion rate and a 4.11 to 1 ROAS.

Built a launch playbook for hospitality: offer design, loyalty programs, and content systems that turn social reach into repeat visits. This refined my approach to positioning, creative direction, and on ground conversion logic.

TechnoMounts - ecommerce and B2B influenced funnel

Ecommerce hardware · United Arab Emirates based brand

Month one delivered 17,500 impressions, 1,510 clicks at 8.6 percent CTR, and 42 conversions across high intent verticals. Organic work lifted impressions from 8,700 to 38,500 and clicks from 320 to 3,250 in a 30 day window.

Here I connected everything: Shopify, GA4, Google Ads, Merchant Center, Search Console, Amazon groundwork, Apollo, and email. My focus shifted from single campaigns to a long term, measurement driven stack.

Across these campaigns I progressed from strong execution on individual tactics to running the entire system. Early work focused on offers, creative, and short term results. Rafiki and Haksoss added layers of automation, CRM, and loyalty economics.

With TechnoMounts I now treat marketing as a connected machine. I am comfortable designing the strategy, building it inside the tools, setting up tracking and dashboards, and then running structured experiments. The result is a portfolio that shows both creative thinking and operational discipline.

How I work

End to end marketing process, owned by one person

I do not only design slides. I can take a project from research and strategy all the way through build, tracking, launch, optimisation, and reporting, working directly inside the tools.

01
Research and positioning
Market mapping, competitor scans, keyword and audience research, and buyer personas. I define where the brand sits and which buyers we need to reach first.
02
Strategy and funnel design
Campaign objectives, channel mix, landing page logic, offers, and lifecycle paths. This is where I turn research into a practical funnel blueprint.
03
Build and integration
Implement campaigns in Google Ads, set up landing pages, connect GA4, Merchant Center, and Search Console, and wire pixel and tag tracking correctly.
04
Content, creative, and offers
Write ad copy, page copy, and emails. Design or brief visuals. Build offer structures, loyalty mechanics, and retention programs that make sense for the brand.
05
Launch, measurement, and reporting
Launch campaigns, monitor learning phases, keep dashboards clean, and report on performance with clear recommendations instead of raw numbers.
06
Iteration and scaling
Run structured tests on audiences, creatives, bids, and pages. Scale what works, fix what does not, and keep the system healthy instead of chasing vanity metrics.
These steps are not abstract. They come from real campaigns like Mory Gym, Rafiki, Haksoss, and TechnoMounts, which forced me to master one discipline at a time: first creative and offers, then funnels and automation, and finally analytics and architecture.

Today I am comfortable joining at any stage. Some brands need a strategy and full build from zero. Others already have accounts and only need structure, clean data, and better decision making. Either way, I can move between high level thinking and detailed execution without getting lost.
