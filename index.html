How my work evolved

From single campaigns to complete marketing systems

Each core campaign added a different building block: creative and offers, automation and retention, loyalty economics, and finally full stack analytics and architecture.

Mory Gym – membership acquisition and retention

Fitness · Alexandria

180,000 reach, 1,800 new members, 18.2 percent engagement, 12 percent conversion, and 4,780 percent ROI across a 12 week campaign.

This campaign taught me how to design offers, funnels, and content that move people from interest to membership, and how to translate creative performance into financial language that decision makers can trust.

Rafiki Pet Hospital – full funnel for appointments

Healthcare · Veterinary hospital

Built a funnel where 540 new leads per month at the awareness stage become around 184 paying clients per month, with repeat visits, referrals, and automation driven retention layers.

Here I focused on marketing automation, lead scoring, CRM integration, and using data from Meta, GA4, and Zoho to understand which channels drive real appointments instead of just traffic.

Haksoss Café – launch and loyalty economics

Hospitality · Premium café

80,800 unique users, 11.3 percent engagement, 6.2 percent conversion, 1,070 loyalty sign ups, and 4.11:1 ROAS with a projected EGP 9.4M lifetime revenue.

This campaign pushed me to think about loyalty, lifetime value, and retention as core parts of marketing, not an afterthought. It refined my approach to funnels, email flows, and incentive structures.

TechnoMounts – ecommerce and B2B influenced stack

Ecommerce hardware · United Arab Emirates

17,500 impressions, 1,510 clicks, 42 conversions, and SEO growth from 8,700 to 38,500 impressions and 320 to 3,250 clicks in 30 days.

TechnoMounts brought everything together: GA4, Google Ads, Merchant Center, Search Console, Shopify, dashboards, and B2B outreach. The focus is now on systems, not single channels.

Each project forced me to master a new layer. Mory Gym sharpened my work on offers, creative, and direct response. Rafiki made me comfortable with automation, CRM, and multi stage funnels. Haksoss added loyalty economics and long term customer value.

With TechnoMounts, I work as a full stack marketer: mapping the buyer’s journey, building campaigns and landing pages, wiring tracking and dashboards, and then running structured experiments. The result is a portfolio that is not built on theory, but on real accounts and measurable outcomes.

How I work

End to end marketing – one person owning the full stack

I am comfortable joining at any point, but my best work happens when I can connect the full chain: research, strategy, build, launch, measurement, and iteration.

01
Research and positioning
Market mapping, competitor analysis, audience research, and personas. I clarify where the brand fits, what buyers care about, and which segments should be prioritised first.
02
Strategy and funnel design
Campaign objectives, channel mix, landing page logic, offers, and lifecycle touchpoints. This is where creative ideas become a practical funnel tied to numbers.
03
Build and technical setup
Implement campaigns in Google Ads, build landing pages, connect GA4, Merchant Center, Search Console, pixels, events, and conversion tracking so that every action is measurable.
04
Content, creative, and offers
Write ads, page copy, and emails. Design or brief visuals, offers, and loyalty mechanics that match how people actually decide, for both B2C and B2B influenced journeys.
05
Launch, measurement, and reporting
Launch campaigns, watch the learning phase, keep dashboards clean, and report back using the fewest possible metrics needed to make good decisions.
06
Iteration, testing, and scaling
Run tests on audiences, creatives, bids, and pages. Scale what works, cut what does not, and keep the underlying setup healthy instead of chasing short term spikes.
This approach is not theoretical. It comes from building and running campaigns like Mory Gym, Rafiki, Haksoss, and TechnoMounts, where the work had to prove itself in real numbers, not just decks.

I am used to moving between strategy and execution, and between creative work and analytics. That combination is what allows this portfolio to show both the story of a campaign and the performance that followed.
