01 - Methodology

From single tasks to complete systems

Every engagement starts with structure: accounts, tracking, and landing pages are built as one engine, not separate checklists.

The priority is to design a machine that can run, improve, and scale without constant reinvention. Each channel plugs into a shared map of audience segments, intent levels, and conversion actions. This makes it possible to know what is happening across campaigns instead of guessing from isolated numbers.

  • Connect acquisition, analytics, and conversion into one performance stack.
  • Use GA4, Google Ads, Search Console, CRM, and landing pages as a unified data source.
  • Structure campaigns so that tests, scaling, and handovers are predictable and documented.
  • Report using the fewest metrics needed for decisions that affect revenue, margin, and pipeline.

05 - SEO

SEO as part of the system, not a checklist

SEO work is designed to support the same structure used by paid media, so both channels reinforce each other.

Methodology

  • Map search intent to specific landing pages instead of pushing all demand to a generic page.
  • Build internal linking across blog posts, collections, and products so visitors move deeper by design.
  • Maintain clean on page structure with titles, meta descriptions, semantic headings, and structured FAQ segments.
  • Monitor Core Web Vitals, crawl issues, index coverage, and structured data so technical debt does not block growth.
  • Use Search Console queries as a source of truth for new content, campaign ideas, and optimisations.

Impact in practice

  • TechnoMounts - organic and paid traffic push into the same set of landing pages, improving blended results.
  • Haksoss Cafe - local and brand queries are served with relevant experiences that support loyalty and repeat visits.
  • Rafiki and Mory Gym - SEO captures demand from people researching clinics and gyms, supporting the paid funnel instead of competing with it.

08 - Full stack

End to end marketing owned by one person

Best work happens when the full chain can be owned in one view instead of breaking it into disconnected tasks.
Step 1 - Research and positioning

Market mapping, competitor analysis, audience research, and personas. Clarify where the brand fits, what buyers care about most, and which segments to prioritise.

Step 2 - Strategy and funnel design

Turn objectives into a funnel with channel mix, landing page logic, offers, and lifecycle touchpoints. Creative ideas are translated into journeys tied to numbers.

Step 3 - Build and technical setup

Implement campaigns in Google Ads, build landing pages, connect GA4, Merchant Center, Search Console, pixels, and conversion tracking so every key action is measurable.

Step 4 - Content, creative, and offers

Write ads, page copy, and emails. Brief or design visuals and loyalty mechanics that reflect how people decide in both B2C and B2B influenced journeys.

Step 5 - Launch, measurement, and reporting

Launch campaigns, manage the learning phase, keep dashboards clean, and report with the fewest metrics needed for confident decisions.

Step 6 - Iteration, testing, and scaling

Design tests on audiences, creatives, bids, and pages. Scale what works, cut what does not, and protect the underlying structure instead of chasing spikes.
